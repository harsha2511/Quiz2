Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. I am currently studying in 3rd year B.A./B.Com./B.Sc. Am I eligible to appear for GATE 2025?

YES, any undergraduate student currently in 3rd year or higher years of any government approved program in Engineering/ Technology/ Architecture/ Science/ Commerce/Arts/ Humanities is eligible to appear for GATE 2025.

2. I am currently studying in 3rd year B.E., but through parallel entry in the 2nd year after finishing Diploma, am I eligible to appear for GATE 2025?

YES, any undergraduate student currently in 3rd year or higher years of any government approved program in Engineering/ Technology/ Architecture/ Science/ Commerce/Arts/ Humanities is eligible to appear for GATE 2025.

3. Is there any age limit to appear for GATE 2025?

NO. There is NO age limit to appear for GATE 2025.

4. Are there any restrictions on the number of times one can appear for GATE?

NO. One can appear for the GATE examination any number of times.

5. Can I appear in any of the GATE 2025 papers?

Although, the candidate is free to choose any of the papers (up to two from the given combinations), candidate should select paper appropriate to the discipline of qualifying degree.

6. How do I apply ONLINE?

Visit https://gate2025.iitr.ac.in/

7. Can I use one e-mail address to fill multiple application forms?

NO. One e-mail address can be used to submit only ONE application form.

8. Do I need to fill two forms to appear in two papers?

NO. The two papers option may be selected based on the given set of combinations in a SINGLE form only.

9. Why should I choose THREE examination cities?

The candidates will most likely be allotted a centre in the examination city of their first choice. Only in cases, where there are too many candidates opting for a certain city as their first choice, the other two choices become relevant.

10. What do I do, if the power/internet connection failed during the submission of application process?

Please login to your account at GATE 2025 website again and continue the application process.
